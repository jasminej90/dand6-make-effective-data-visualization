Gender Equality in Numbers

in the Arab World

Global Gender Gap Index (GGGI) seeks to measure an aspect of gender equality:

the relative gaps between women and men, across a large set of countries and across four key areas: health, education, economics and politics. The highest possible score is 1 (equality) and the lowest possible score is 0 (inequality).

First, How's the World Doing in Terms of Closing the Gap Between the Two Genders?

It's worth noting that as of 2016, no country has succeeded yet in closing the gap between the two genders by 100%. Nonetheless, about 21 countries managed to close gap by 75% or more.

What are the Arab World Rankings in 2016?

Arab countries have managed to close 64% - 51% of the gender gap in 2016. Qatar comes at the front in terms of closing the gap between the two genders in health, education, economics and politics areas, however, it's ranked 119 worldwide. Yemen ranked last in both Arab and worldwide regions with a score of 0.516

Did those Rankings of the Arab World Change Over a Period of Seven Years [2007 - 2013]?

Some of the countries that are clearly progressing on closing the gender gap over time are: Saudi Arabia, Morocco, UAE, Egypt, Qatar (0.604 to 0.643 in a period of 9 years), Oman, Algeria, Yemen (0.451 - 0.516 in a period of 9 years), Bahrain, Mauritania (0.581 in 2013 - to 0.624 in 2016). On the other hand, some countries are underperforming such as, Kuwait (started with a 0.641 GGGI in 2007, but decreased to 0.624 in 2016), Jordan, and Syria.

Data Source: World Economic Forum Global Gender Gap Report 2016
